This resource addresses civil law only. It does not challenge Catholic doctrine on sacramental marriage. It focuses on the state's duty to protect legal rights, children, and personal dignity when a marriage is irreparably broken.
Why This Matters in Practice

The absence of absolute divorce does not prevent marital breakdown. It changes who is allowed to recover from it. Under the current framework, victims remain trapped in abusive marriages while the poor are denied access to legal remedies.
