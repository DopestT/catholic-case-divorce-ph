Overview

This resource presents a Catholic-grounded civil-law analysis of the Philippine Absolute Divorce Bill, designed to support legislators, congressional staff, and stakeholders with credible information rooted in Church teaching, Philippine law, and national statistics.

The goal is to provide accessible, evidence-based material that respects Catholic doctrine while addressing the civil and social dimensions of the proposed legislation.

Key Analysis Points

Catholic Social Teaching Perspective

The Catholic Church upholds the indissolubility of sacramental marriage as a fundamental tenet. However, the Church also recognizes the complexity of civil law and the need to protect vulnerable individuals, particularly women and children, in situations of marital breakdown.

This analysis examines how civil divorce legislation intersects with:

  • The dignity of the human person
  • The common good
  • Protection of family life
  • Social justice considerations

Philippine Legal Framework

The Philippines remains one of the few countries globally without absolute divorce provisions. Current options include:

  • Annulment under civil law
  • Legal separation
  • Declaration of nullity of marriage

The proposed Absolute Divorce Bill aims to provide an additional legal remedy for irretrievably broken marriages, with specific grounds and procedural safeguards.

National Statistics & Social Impact

Understanding the social context is essential for informed policy decisions. Key considerations include:

  • Prevalence of de facto separation in Philippine society
  • Economic vulnerability of abandoned spouses
  • Child welfare in broken families
  • Access to legal remedies across economic classes
